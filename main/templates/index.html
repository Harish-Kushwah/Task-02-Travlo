Historical Places

Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir, also known as the Ayodhya Temple, is a partially constructed Hindu temple complex located in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India.

Key Features

  • Location: Ram Janmabhoomi, Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Style: Nagara style of temple architecture
  • Features: Five mandapas, garbhagriha, shikhara, and a tower at the top
  • Significance: Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, a principal deity of Hinduism

Jal Mahal

Jal Mahal is a palace located in the middle of the Man Sagar Lake in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. The palace was constructed in 1699 and was later renovated and enlarged in the 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Amber.

Key Features

  • al Mahal is often referred to as the “Water Palace” due to its unique location in the middle of the lake.
  • The palace was built as a summer retreat for the royal family and was used for hunting and recreation.
  • The palace's architecture is designed to allow cool air to circulate through the structure, making it a comfortable retreat from the hot summer temperatures.
  • Jal Mahal is a popular spot for boating and picnics, and offers stunning views of the surrounding city and the surrounding hills.
